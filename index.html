While it is important to focus in preparing the future generations of farmers and other agriculture stakeholders, it is equally important to equip the future ICT innovators that will support them. AgriHackathon 3 calls on to the pillars of ICT education in the country to become partners in molding young minds as ICT-AgrInnovators. As the direction of global agriculture leans toward precision thru the use of ICT, they will be one of the key players who will take part in making the Philippines ahead of other developing countries in producing enough and affordable food.

OBJECTIVE:

To equip the future ICT-AgrInnovators with knowledge, experience, and motivation to develop ICT support for agriculture by:

  • Challenging and recognizing ideas, creativity, and innovation skills
  • Educating them about the direction of Philippine agriculture and the different opportunities in ICT for agriculture
  • Promoting business startups on ICT services for agriculture

Who can join? All State Universities and Colleges (SUC) in Luzon with ICT-related BS degree are invited to send their student-representatives to join the hackathon.

Challenge Mechanics

  1. Build your teams. SUCs have the option to register up to two teams. Each team must be composed of three students and an accompanying instructor-coach. All student-delegates must belong to the graduating class of 2nd semester 2017.

  2. Prepare your concept note. Prepare your concept note. Using this format, prepare a concept note of your ICT-based agricultural solution. We understand that some of you might find it difficult to generate idea. Here’s a list of themes and references that you can use.

  3. Submit your details. Once you have teams and concept note ready, register here. Entry submisssion will close on March 21, 2017 at 12MN.

  4. Pre-work your prototype. In doing this, gather as much help as you need from your school. Coaches, you are encouraged to assist your team in consulting with agriculture professors and instructors in your respective universities.

    Changes in team composition beyond the closing time of the

    5. registration page will be considered but must be justified. Likewise, modifications in your solution concept will be accepted given that it did not alter too far from the original idea. You must inform the committee the soonest upon revision.


  5. Get PhilRice experts’ advice. Before the start of the hackathon proper, PhilRice researchers will be given one hour to roam around and check your concept and prototype. You may consult them for suggestions or clarifications in order to improve your projects.

  6. Let the hackathon begin. Teams will be given 24 hours to improve and enhance their prototype. All the work will be done inside a designated room. Instructor-coaches are allowed to join their team throughout the hackathon.

  7. Pitch with all your heart. You will be given 10 minutes to pitch your prototype to a panel of judges. A guide questions will be provided to help you comprehensively discuss your solution. Only the students can present.

Prizes

1st Place: 5-month internship program worth PhP225,000.00 (PhP75,000.00/student) + PhP30,000.00 budget for supplies to improve and expand their prototype + PhP10,000.00 cash + medal + other perks + trophy for the school

2nd and 3rd Place: Medal + perks + will be considered for possible internship

Perks. All participants will get event shirt and ID lace. Special prizes will be raffled during the hackathon proper. Coffee? Snacks? Meals? We have them for you. A place to sleep? Nah, you won’t need it. It’s a hackathon. Coaches can stay in the PhilRice-Hostel.

Entries that have already won awards (1st, 2nd, or 3rd) in any international or national competitions, or those that are already used and available in the market will not be allowed to be used in this competition in order to give fair chance to other ideas and developers.
